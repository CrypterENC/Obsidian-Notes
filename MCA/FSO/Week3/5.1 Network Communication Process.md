### What is Network ? 
- A **network** is a **~={green}group of devices connected together=~** so they can **~={green}share information or resources.=~**
### Networks are of many Sizes.
-  Networks ~={yellow}range from=~ **~={cyan}just 2 computers=~** **~={cyan}all the way up to millions of devices=~** worldwide.

- **Why Organizations Use Networks:**
	- ~={yellow}Consolidate resources=~
	- ~={yellow}Store data centrally=~
	- ~={yellow}Share access to information=~ on servers
	
#### Types of Networks for diff sizes : 
1. **Peer to peer Network:**
	- In small office or homes, many **~={green}computers function as both the servers and clients=~** on the network
2. **Small Home Network:**
	- Here **~={green}it connects a few computer to each other=~** and **~={green}to the internet.=~**
3. **Small Office and Home office:**
	- This networks **~={green}allows a home office or a remote office to connect to a corporate network=~.**
4. **Medium to large networks:**
	- They are **~={green}used in corporations and schools=~** and **~={green}can have many locations with hundreds and thousands of interconnected hosts.=~**
5. **World Wide Networks:**
	- This internet is a **~={green}network of networks that connects hundreds of millions of computer world wide.=~**

### Client-Server Communications
#### What are Host ? 
- All computers **~={green}connected to a network that participate in network communication=~** are classified **~={cyan}as hosts=~**. 
- Hosts are also called **end devices, endpoints, or nodes.**
#### What are Servers ? 
- **~={green}Servers=~** are **~={yellow}computers with specialized software.=~** 
- This **~={green}software enables=~** servers to **~={cyan}provide information to other end devices on the network.=~** 
#### Types of Servers :
- A server can be 
	- Single-purpose Servers - **providing only one service**, such as web pages. 
	- Multipurpose, **providing a variety of services** 

##### Services provided by the server
- **File Server** - The file server **~={blue}stores corporate and user files in a central location.=~** The client devices **access these files with client software such as Windows explorer** 
	
- **Web Server** - The web server runs web server software and clients use their browser software such as Windows Internet explorer that allows many computers to access web pages. 
	
- **Email Server** - The email server runs email server software that enables emails to be sent and received.

### Why networking is imp to cybersecurity experts.

> We use the **internet every day without thinking about how it works** — like driving a car without knowing what's under the hood.

- But for **cybersecurity analysts**, understanding how data travels is **essential**.

- **Data can take many paths** meaning `Traffic from ur computer (client) can take many path to reach to the server.`

	- Your data is ~={yellow}broken down into smaller pieces called **packets**=~. 
	
		- Each ~={yellow}**packet** is like a postcard =~with a ~={green}"from" address (your IP) and a "to" address (the website's IP). =~
		- These packets ~={yellow}don't follow a single, **pre-defined route**=~. Instead, they are handed off from **one networking device** (a router) **to the next**, with **~={cyan}each router independently deciding the best way to send the packet=~** toward its final destination.
- **Traffic routes are unpredictable:** Your **~={yellow}data might travel far=~** in ~={green}one direction=~, **~={blue}then turn around and go somewhere else to reach its destination.=~** The path it takes to get there might be completely different from the path it takes to return.
		
- **The Structure of the Internet:**  To manage this **massive, complex flow of data,** the **~={green}internet is structured in a loose hierarchy of networks.=~**

	- **~={orange}Global Tier 1 and Tier 2 ISPs=~** ~={purple}~={blue}**form the backbone of the internet**=~ and connect to each other=~ at **places called** **~={yellow}Internet Exchange Points (IXPs)=~**
		-  **~={red}Larger networks connect to ~={orange}Tier 2 network=~**=~ with the help of **something called** as **~={cyan}Point of Presence(PoP)=~**.
			-  **~={cyan}PoP=~** is a **location in the building** **~={green}where physical connections are made to the ISP=~.**
	- **~={orange}Tier 3 ISPs=~** are the **local providers** ~={cyan}which connect homes and businesses=~

- - **Why this matters for cybersecurity**: **~={green}Analysts need to trace where traffic comes from and where it’s going.=~** Understanding the path data takes **~={cyan}helps them spot threats and protect the network.=~**
